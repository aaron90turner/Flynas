TURKEY ATTRACTIONS

There are a number of must-see attractions in Turkey including Gallipoli, the site of ANZAC Cove, the city of Troy, and Istanbul’s Blue Mosque, Basilica Cistern, Topkapi Palace, Hagia Sophia, the Grand Bazaar and Taksim Square. At most of the attractions you can sign up for guided tours if you wish to be educated about the historical importance of the site

SHOP TURKEY

There are great souvenirs to pick up in Turkey including boxes of Turkish Delight, hand-painted ceramics, herbs and spices and fashionable items. So head to one of the many souks, shopping streets or malls.

EATING OUT IN TURKEY

There is an abundance of fresh local food available in Turkey, making this a prime tourist destination for food connoisseurs. Fruits such as figs and pomegranate are locally grown and the sea provides vast amounts of fresh seafood too. The local cuisine also consists of favourites including kofte (a meat dish), doner kebabs, dolma (stuffed vegetables) and meze. For those with a sweet tooth, the Turkish delight and baklava is a must try along with Turkish coffee.

TURKEY HOTELS

Whichever city you have chosen to visit for work of holiday, there is a huge range of hotels to choose from including 3 to 5-star accommodation.

WEATHER IN TURKEY

Most of Turkey enjoys warm summers of 29 degrees and cool winters at 5 degrees. However if you can bear the cold then visiting the country in the cooler months has its benefits including less tourists and shorter queues.

EVENTS IN TURKEY

There are hundreds of events held across Turkey every year however some of the more popular include the Istanbul Shopping Festival (March to April), ANZAC Day in Gallipoli (April), Bosphorus Cross-Continental Swim (July), Rock ‘n Coke (September), ArtInternational Istanbul (September), Akbank Jazz Festival (September to October), Istanbul Biennale (September to October), NBA Global Games (October), WTA Tennis Championships (October) and Istanbul Design Week (November to December).
